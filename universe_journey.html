The verdict

Cameron was essentially correct

When you plug the universe's mass into the black hole equation, you get a Schwarzschild radius roughly the size of... the observable universe.

This is a known result in cosmology — and it's not a coincidence. The Friedmann equations (which describe how the universe expands) are structurally identical to the escape velocity equation.

The universe sits right at the critical density threshold. It has the mass budget of a black hole — but expansion is running the other direction. Dark energy is pushing outward faster than gravity can pull inward.

Cameron's "pressure trying to refill" intuition? That's the right direction. The tension between them is what we're living inside.
