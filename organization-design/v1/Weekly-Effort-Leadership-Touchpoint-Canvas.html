Weekly Effort Leadership Touchpoint

Leadership discussion guide for active initiatives
Purpose:
Provide leadership visibility into effort health, progress, emerging issues, and areas where guidance or decisions may be needed.

This is not: a task review or replacement for operational team meetings.

1. Context

"What are we trying to accomplish?"
  • Purpose of effort
  • Desired outcome
  • Stakeholders involved
  • Current priority

2. Current State

"Where are we now?"
  • Overall status
  • Progress since last discussion
  • Major changes
  • Important updates

3. Key Progress

"What moved forward?"
  • Accomplishments
  • Decisions made
  • Stakeholder engagements
  • Completed activities

4. Challenges

"Where do we need attention?"
  • Persistent blockers
  • Dependencies
  • Risks
  • Leadership awareness needed

5. Looking Ahead

"What is coming next?"
  • Upcoming decisions
  • Near-term priorities
  • Expected engagements
  • Potential concerns

6. Leadership Guidance

"What do we need from leadership?"
  • Direction
  • Priority alignment
  • Escalation support
  • Context from leadership

Good Weekly Discussion

  • Focused on decisions and awareness
  • Creates shared understanding
  • Surfaces issues early
  • Allows leadership to add value

Avoid Becoming

  • Long status readout
  • Task-by-task update
  • Problem-solving meeting
  • Duplicate of working sessions
Recommended duration: 30–45 minutes per effort or workstream depending on complexity.
