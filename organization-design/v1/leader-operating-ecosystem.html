The shape above the tool map

Leader Operating Ecosystem

One page, six pieces. This is the trunk — your leader's role, what they're actually on the hook for, and how information should move around them. Every worksheet and canvas in the tool map is a drill-down into one of these six pieces, not a separate thing. Fill it in loosely first; precision comes later, from the linked tools.

The one-liner — say this out loud, refine until it's true
The leader exists to hold decision authority for AI and data governance across the organization — the team develops the options, the leader decides and promulgates them.
Mandate→ Decision Surface→ Information Diet→ Support Structure→ External Interfaces→ Maturity Signals
01

Mandate

Why does this role exist, and on what authority?
A senior governance and technology leadership role. Authority anchored in the organization's data-governance and AI-governance policy. Serves as a translator and operational counterpart to senior leadership — carries their intent down, and represents the team's readiness up. The team exists to develop the work product the leader needs in order to exercise this mandate well; the team does not hold the authority itself.
Feeds from / anchors
Org data/AI governance policy (policy, not a tool) The team's own operating model (Architect v3)
02

Decision Surface

What is the leader actually on the hook to decide or promulgate — and nothing more?
Sign-off on team-recommended governance frameworks and role assignments (e.g., expanding a team member's scope). Resolution of flagged ownership gaps (e.g., a budget/resourcing owner, still open). Leadership-review decks and similar artifacts, once the team has drafted them. The test for what belongs here: they have to decide it, they'll be asked about it, or something changed materially. Everything else should never reach this list.
Feeds from / drills into
Team State of Play (weekly decision sheet) Role Alignment Canvas
03

Information Diet

What reaches the leader, how often, and in what format — so nothing arrives as a surprise?
Weekly: State of Play, refreshed in standup (Mon/Wed/Fri). As-needed: team recommendations and briefs ahead of a decision. If a monthly strategic touchpoint is adopted: PMR Lite pre-read + discussion. Per-initiative: weekly effort syncs where they exist. Still to define: the minimum they get even when "nothing changed," and the exact escalation channel for anything urgent and off-cadence.
Feeds from / drills into
Team Cadence Builder (design the loop) Monthly Touchpoint suite (0→3→1→2) Weekly Effort Touchpoint
04

Support Structure

Who serves this mandate, and where are the gaps still open?
The team (contractor support role — two Technical SMEs, two Portfolio Managers, one Comms Specialist) develops the work product the leader decides on. Under discussion: expanding a team member's role to own AI capability orchestration on the leader's behalf — connective tissue across cyber/data/risk, arming the leader with frameworks before deployments. Open gap: no owner yet for token pricing / AI portfolio management or broader budget planning (that team member's certification doesn't cover budgeting).
Feeds from / drills into
Team Operating Model (Architect v3) Role-expansion alignment conversation (template, not in this set) Budget / token-pricing owner — unresolved
05

External Interfaces

Who does the leader coordinate across, and what does each relationship need from them?
Senior leadership above them (chain-of-command discipline matters; routing goes through the proper chain, not around it). Cyber, data, and risk stakeholders for AI capability vetting. Org-wide counterparts operating under the same governance mandate.
Feeds from / drills into
Stakeholder Engagement section (Architect v3)
06

Maturity Signals

A year from now, how will you know this ecosystem is working?
Fewer ad hoc, off-cadence asks. Items that reach the leader's decision surface are genuinely theirs to decide — nothing trivial gets through. The team surfaces reality early rather than needing rescue (recoverability over perfection). Rhythm feels predictable rather than reactive, to the leader and to the team.
The contract, restated

The team develops and recommends. The leader decides and promulgates. Every tool in the drill-down map exists to make one of the six pieces above sharper — not to replace this page. If a new tool doesn't obviously feed one of these six blocks, that's worth a second look before you build it.
