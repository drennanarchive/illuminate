A living plan for turning a simple sleep checklist into a small, magical self-care experience Clara actually wants to open.

North Star

We are not making a habit tracker. We are asking: how do we make self-care feel magical?

Current Design Maturity

Prototype is functional. The next work is experience, polish, and delight.

Things We Must Protect

These are the current success conditions and bug fixes that should survive every future iteration.

🛡️

Reset Reliability

Reset must clear immediately and never trigger completion by accident.

Keep the reset guard pattern: state clears first, update runs silently, completion checks only happen after intentional user toggles.
Critical
🌙

No-Guilt Language

The app welcomes. It never scolds, shames, or pressures.

Avoid broken streaks, failure language, productivity-dashboard vibes, and anything that makes missing a day feel like moral failure.
Design Pillar
📱

Mobile Smoothness

Mobile should feel cozy, app-like, and not overloaded.

Use fewer, better particles. Keep heavy atmosphere mostly on desktop. Respect reduced motion and large touch targets.

Testing Scorecard

Use this after each prototype. The goal is not merely “does it work?” The goal is “does Clara want to open it?”

⚡

Performance

Does it feel smooth on phone?

If mobile lags, reduce particles before reducing magic. Replace quantity with shape, timing, and layering.
🖥️

Distinct Vibes

Does desktop feel more cinematic than mobile?

Desktop can be expansive and atmospheric. Mobile should be compact, cozy, and touch-friendly.
🔊

Sound Feel

Does the shimmer feel magical instead of sharp?

Aim for 0.8–1.5 seconds: tiny bell, high sparkle, glass resonance, airy shimmer tail.
“Every interaction should feel like adding light to the night sky rather than checking work off a list.”
