Strategy framework reference

Four terms, one through-line

Strategic Outcome → Outcome → KPI + Milestones

Strategic Outcome

2–3 per directorate

The big, directorate-level destination — what the entire directorate exists to move toward. Broad enough to hold every team's work underneath it.

Example

"Decision-makers across the enterprise get trustworthy data when they need it, not after the fact."

— how a data & analytics directorate might frame it

Outcome

3–8 per Strategic Outcome

A significant shift the organization is on the hook to deliver within 3 years — substantial enough to carry real weight at any layer of leadership, not a single team's task list.

This is where the Strategic Outcome becomes accountable and concrete.

Example

"Establish a single authoritative data pipeline that every directorate reports against, replacing today's fragmented, directorate-specific sources."

— how a reporting & analysis line might frame it

Both track progress on one Outcome

KPI

1 per Outcome

The specific, quantitative measurement tracked the whole way through — paired with a threshold that defines what "off track" looks like, not just a number to watch.

Example

"Average data request turnaround time, tracked monthly. Threshold: flag if turnaround exceeds 5 business days for two consecutive months."

Milestone

3 required — one per year

What success looks like by the end of a given year on the way to the Outcome. All three years are required — together they form the full path, not a single checkpoint to pick from.

YEAR 1 Automate intake
YEAR 2 Pilot self-service dashboard
YEAR 3 Full directorate rollout

If you're filling this out: start at Outcome. Ask "what are we on the hook to deliver in 3 years," then "what measurement tells us we're on pace, and at what point is that measurement a problem," then "what does success look like at the end of each of the next 3 years."
