Strategy Framework Reference

Four Terms, One Through-Line

Strategic Outcome → Outcome → KPI + Milestones
One page · plain language · no jargon

2–3 per directorate

Strategic
Outcome

The big, directorate-level destination — what the entire directorate exists to move toward. Broad enough to hold multiple teams' work underneath it.

Example

"Decision-makers get trustworthy data when they need it, not after."

— how a data/analytics shop might frame it

3–8 per Strategic Outcome

Outcome

What a specific team is on the hook to accomplish over the next 3 years. Specific enough to own; still describes a result, not a task list.

This is where the Strategic Outcome gets real.

Example

"Cut the time from data request to delivered report from 2 weeks to 2 days."

— how a reporting/analyst team might frame it

1 per Outcome

KPI

The single number you watch the whole way through. Tells you, at any point, whether you're on track to hit the Outcome — or drifting off it.

Example

"Average turnaround time per request, tracked monthly."

3 per Outcome · 1 per year

Milestone

What "on track" looks like by the end of this year specifically — a checkpoint, not the finish line.

Example

"Yr 1: automate intake. Yr 2: pilot self-service dashboard. Yr 3: full rollout."

KPI and Milestones sit at the same level — both measure progress on one Outcome

If you're filling this out: start at Outcome. Ask "what are we on the hook for in 3 years," then "what number tells us we're on pace," then "what does year one look like."
